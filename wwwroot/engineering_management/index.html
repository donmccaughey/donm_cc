Engineering Management

In 2017 I became an engineering manager, a career change I wasn't looking for and hadn't previously considered. My experience as consultant, tech lead and member of Extreme Programming teams helped in the transition, but there is still a lot to learn in my new role.

Management is a skill quite different from software engineering, and I still consider myself a beginner. This page includes some of the things I've found useful.

Practical

  • Manager Tools "Basics", Michael Auzenne and Mark Horstman, 2005
  • Crucial Conversations: Tools for Talking When Stakes Are High, Kerry Patterson, Joseph Grenny, Ron McMillan and Al Switzler, 2011
  • Crucial Accountability: Tools for Resolving Violated Expectations, Broken Commitments and Bad Behavior, Kerry Patterson, Joseph Grenny, Ron McMillan and Al Switzler, 2013
  • Ask a Manager, Alison Green

Engineering Management

  • Software Lead Weekly: A weekly email for busy people who care about people, culture and leadership, Oren Ellenbogen
  • The Manager's Path: A Guide for Tech Leaders Navigating Growth and Change, Camille Fournier, 2017
  • Irrational Exuberance!, Will Larson

Teamwork

  • The Five Dysfunctions of a Team: A Leadership Fable, Patrick Lencioni, 2011
  • The Ideal Team Player: How to Recognize and Cultivate the Three Essential Virtues, Patrick Lencioni, 2016

Leadership

  • Turn the Ship Around!: A True Story of Turning Followers into Leaders, L David Marquet, 2013
  • The Four Obsessions of an Extraordinary Executive: A Leadership Fable, Patrick Lencioni, 2008

Theoretical

  • Measuring and Managing Performance in Organizations, Robert D Austin, 1996
  • Don't Shoot the Dog: The Art of Teaching and Training, Karen Pryor, 1984
